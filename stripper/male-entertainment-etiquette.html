Male Entertainment Etiquette Guide

Creating respectful, enjoyable experiences for everyone involved

Hiring entertainment for your celebration should be fun, exciting, and respectful for everyone involved. Understanding proper etiquette ensures the performer feels valued, your guests feel comfortable, and the entire experience remains positive from start to finish.

Whether you're planning a bachelorette party, birthday celebration, or girls night out, these etiquette guidelines help create an environment where entertainment enhances the celebration rather than creating awkward or uncomfortable situations.

The Core Principle

Entertainers are professionals providing a service, not objects for your personal gratification. Treating performers with the same respect you'd show any other service provider ensures positive experiences and maintains their dignity while still allowing everyone to have fun.

Before Booking

Pre-Event Etiquette

DO:

  • Research reputable services: Look for established businesses with positive reviews and professional websites. Understanding the difference between professional and amateur performers helps ensure quality service.
  • Communicate clearly: Discuss your expectations, event type, group size, venue details, and any special requests upfront.
  • Ask about boundaries: Professional services will explain what is and isn't included in performances.
  • Provide accurate information: Be honest about guest count, venue type, and event duration.
  • Understand pricing: Review typical entertainment costs to budget appropriately and avoid payment disputes.

DON'T:

  • Negotiate inappropriately: Don't try to barter services for services or make uncomfortable offers.
  • Book through sketchy channels: Avoid random social media DMs or unverified contacts.
  • Make assumptions: Don't assume what's included without asking directly.
  • Hide important details: Don't conceal difficult venue access, large group sizes, or other relevant information.
Day of Event

Event Day Etiquette

Arrival & Setup

DO:

  • Provide private changing area: Offer a bathroom, bedroom, or private space for the performer to prepare and store belongings.
  • Greet professionally: Welcome the entertainer warmly but professionally upon arrival.
  • Brief performer on group: Share any relevant information about attendees that might affect the performance.
  • Ensure clear performance space: Have furniture moved and space ready before arrival.
  • Offer refreshments: Provide water and access to bathroom facilities.

DON'T:

  • Start drinking heavily before arrival: Maintain composure to communicate effectively with performer.
  • Be unprepared: Don't scramble to move furniture or clear space after the entertainer arrives.
  • Make them wait: Don't keep the performer waiting while you finish other activities.
  • Invade privacy: Don't enter the changing area without permission.
During Performance

Performance Etiquette

Physical Boundaries

Critical Boundary Rules

The performer controls all physical contact. They will initiate any interaction that's part of the performance. Never grab, touch, or make contact with the entertainer unless they clearly invite it as part of the show.

DO:

  • Let the performer lead: They'll guide audience interaction appropriately.
  • Follow their cues: If they invite participation, respond accordingly but respectfully.
  • Enjoy the show: Cheer, laugh, and have fun within appropriate bounds.
  • Respect "no" signals: If someone declines participation, support their choice.
  • Take photos/videos if allowed: Ask permission first and respect any restrictions.
  • Tip generously: Show appreciation for quality entertainment with appropriate tips.

NEVER:

  • Touch without invitation: Keep hands to yourself unless the performer explicitly invites contact.
  • Make degrading comments: No objectifying remarks, explicit requests, or demeaning language.
  • Attempt to remove clothing: This is completely off-limits and potentially illegal.
  • Block the performance: Don't crowd the performer or obstruct others' views.
  • Make sexual advances: Don't proposition the entertainer or make explicit offers.
  • Record without permission: Don't secretly film or photograph if the performer prohibits it.

Alcohol & Behavior Management

While celebrating with drinks is common, excessive intoxication often leads to boundary violations. As the organizer, monitor your group's alcohol consumption and intervene if anyone becomes inappropriate.

Key Responsibility: If someone in your group violates boundaries or makes the performer uncomfortable, you must address it immediately. Don't let bad behavior slide because "they're drunk" or "just having fun."
After Performance

Post-Performance Etiquette

DO:

  • Express genuine appreciation: Thank the performer for their work and professionalism.
  • Provide tip promptly: Have cash tips ready and present them respectfully.
  • Allow private packing time: Let the performer collect belongings and change privately.
  • Leave positive reviews: If service was excellent, share your experience to help the business.
  • Maintain professional distance: Don't attempt to extend the interaction beyond the booked service.

DON'T:

  • Request personal contact information: Don't ask for phone numbers, social media, or personal details.
  • Pressure for additional services: The booked service is complete; don't push for more.
  • Follow or stalk: Never follow a performer to their vehicle or try to learn where they're going next.
  • Post without permission: Don't tag performers in social media posts without consent.
  • Withhold agreed payment: Never shortchange or refuse payment for services rendered.

Managing Your Group's Behavior

As the organizer, you're responsible for your guests' behavior. Set expectations before the entertainer arrives.

Pre-Performance Group Brief

Before the performer arrives, gather your group and establish ground rules:

  • "Let's all have fun but remember this is a professional providing entertainment"
  • "The performer controls all physical contact - hands off unless invited"
  • "We're here to celebrate, not make anyone uncomfortable"
  • "Respect all boundaries and follow the performer's lead"
  • "Anyone who crosses lines will be asked to leave"

Know Your Group

If you have guests who tend to get overly rowdy when drinking or who have boundary issues, consider whether this entertainment type is appropriate for your group. It's better to choose different entertainment than to create an uncomfortable situation.

Handling Special Situations

If Someone Crosses a Line

If a guest violates boundaries or makes the performer uncomfortable:

  1. Intervene immediately: Don't wait or hope it resolves itself
  2. Remove the guest: Ask them to step outside or leave the party
  3. Apologize to performer: Make clear this behavior doesn't represent the group
  4. Offer to end early: Let the performer leave if they feel unsafe
  5. Still pay fully: Compensate the performer completely regardless of early ending

If You Feel Uncomfortable

If the performer crosses boundaries or makes you uncomfortable:

  • Speak up clearly: Use clear language like "I'm not comfortable with that"
  • Remove yourself: Step away from the immediate performance area
  • Tell the organizer: Let them know if you need the performance to stop
  • Remember you can leave: You're never obligated to stay for entertainment

Reputable entertainment services train performers on reading comfort levels and maintaining appropriate boundaries. If a performer consistently ignores discomfort, this is a service quality issue to address with the company.

Venue-Specific Etiquette

Private Residence

  • Clear parking instructions in advance
  • Ensure pets are secured
  • Provide clear entrance directions
  • Have changing area ready
  • Secure valuables in locked rooms

Hotel/Vacation Rental

  • Verify entertainment is allowed in venue rules
  • Provide room number and building access codes
  • Be mindful of noise for other guests
  • Tip housekeeping extra after the party
  • Clean up adequately before checkout

Payment & Tipping Etiquette

Standard Payment Practices

Base Fee: Pay the agreed-upon service fee in full, regardless of performance length within the booked time. If the performance ends early due to guest misbehavior, full payment is still owed.

Tipping: Tips are customary for quality service. Standard is 15-25% of the service fee, with higher amounts for exceptional performances. Have cash ready as many entertainers prefer cash tips.

Pro Tip: Collect tip money from attendees beforehand rather than passing a collection during or after the performance. This avoids awkward money handling and ensures appropriate compensation.

Why This Etiquette Matters

Following proper etiquette when hiring entertainment isn't just about being polite - it has real consequences for everyone involved:

Safety

Respectful behavior creates safe working environments for performers and comfortable experiences for guests.

Quality Service

Professional treatment encourages performers to give their best, creating better experiences for everyone.

Industry Reputation

Respectful clients support legitimate entertainment businesses and help eliminate exploitative practices.

The Bottom Line

Entertainment should enhance celebrations, not create discomfort or unsafe situations. Treating performers with dignity, respecting boundaries, and managing your group's behavior ensures everyone - performers and guests alike - has positive experiences.

When you book entertainment through reputable services, follow these etiquette guidelines, and approach the experience with respect and maturity, you create memorable celebrations that everyone can feel good about afterward. That's what truly great parties are made of.
