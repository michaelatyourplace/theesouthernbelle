Ultimate Bachelorette Party Planning Checklist

Your complete step-by-step guide to planning an unforgettable celebration

Planning a bachelorette party can feel overwhelming with so many details to coordinate. From selecting entertainment to booking venues and managing RSVPs, this comprehensive checklist will guide you through every step of the planning process to ensure a celebration the bride will never forget.

6-8 Months Before

Initial Planning Phase

Talk to the Bride

Have an honest conversation about her vision, comfort level, budget expectations, and must-have elements. Discuss whether she wants a surprise or prefers to be involved in planning decisions.

Set the Budget

Determine a per-person budget that the group can afford. Consider accommodation, transportation, meals, activities, and entertainment costs. Be transparent with all attendees early.

Create Guest List

Work with the bride to finalize who should be invited. Send save-the-date notices to give guests maximum notice for travel planning and budget preparation.

Choose Location & Dates

Select destination based on bride's preferences, guest convenience, and budget. Check major holidays and wedding season availability. Consider weather patterns for outdoor activities.

4-6 Months Before

Booking & Reservations

Book Accommodations

Reserve vacation rental, hotel rooms, or resort space for the group. Look for properties with common areas for gathering, kitchen access, and proximity to planned activities.

Secure Entertainment

Book professional entertainment well in advance, especially for peak seasons. Research entertainers' credentials, read reviews, and verify licensing. Understanding the difference between professional and amateur entertainers ensures you hire quality talent.

Reserve Activity Spots

Book spa appointments, dinner reservations, boat tours, or other planned group activities. Many popular activities require advance booking for large groups.

Arrange Transportation

Book party bus, limo service, or rental vehicles if needed. Consider transportation between accommodation and venues, especially if alcohol will be involved.

2-3 Months Before

Details & Coordination

Send Formal Invitations

Send detailed invitations with full itinerary, costs breakdown, what to pack, and RSVP deadline. Include accommodation details and any special instructions.

Plan the Theme & Decorations

Choose party theme, coordinate matching outfits if desired, order decorations, custom items, and party favors. Order bride's special accessories like sash, veil, or tiara.

Create Detailed Itinerary

Map out the full schedule with timing for each activity. Build in flexibility for spontaneous fun but ensure booked activities are clearly scheduled. For comprehensive activity ideas, check out our girls night entertainment guide.

Budget for Entertainment Costs

Finalize entertainment expenses and communicate costs to attendees. Understanding typical entertainment pricing helps with accurate budgeting.

1 Month Before

Final Preparations

Confirm All RSVPs

Get final headcount and collect outstanding deposits or payments. Follow up with anyone who hasn't responded to ensure accurate numbers for all bookings.

Reconfirm All Bookings

Contact accommodation, restaurants, entertainment, and activities to reconfirm reservations with final guest count. Get confirmation numbers for everything.

Prepare Games & Activities

Purchase or create any games, scavenger hunts, or planned activities. Print materials needed and organize supplies in advance.

Review Entertainment Guidelines

Familiarize yourself with proper entertainment etiquette to ensure everyone knows appropriate behavior and boundaries during the celebration.

Order Supplies

Purchase decorations, party favors, food and beverage supplies, first aid kit, and any specialty items needed. Order custom items like matching shirts or accessories.

1 Week Before

Last-Minute Details

Send Final Details

Email complete itinerary, addresses, contact numbers, parking information, and what to bring to all attendees. Create a group chat for easy day-of communication.

Pack Emergency Kit

Prepare bag with safety pins, stain remover, pain relievers, bandages, phone chargers, snacks, water bottles, and other essentials.

Delegate Responsibilities

Assign specific tasks to reliable attendees - photographer, bar tab monitor, decoration setup, breakfast organizer, etc. Don't try to do everything yourself.

Prepare Payments

Organize cash tips for entertainers, servers, and drivers. Have credit card ready for any unexpected expenses. Know who owes what and have a system to collect.

Day Before

Final 24 Hours

Triple Check Everything

Call all vendors one final time to confirm timing, addresses, and details. Verify entertainment arrival time and location access.

Prepare Venue

If hosting at private location, set up decorations, organize bar area, arrange seating, test music system, and ensure space is clean and ready.

Get Good Sleep

Rest up so you can fully enjoy and manage the celebration. Hydrate well and eat a good meal.

Day Of Party

Celebration Day

Arrive Early

Get to venue or accommodation early to handle any last-minute setup, greet early arrivals, and ensure everything is ready.

Welcome Entertainment

Greet entertainer upon arrival, show them changing area, discuss timing and any special requests. Have payment ready.

Document Everything

Assign someone to take photos and videos throughout the celebration. Capture special moments, group shots, and candid fun.

Stay Flexible & Have Fun

Things may not go exactly as planned - that's okay! Stay adaptable, keep positive energy, and focus on creating amazing memories with the bride.

Pro Planning Tips

Budget Smart

Break down costs clearly and collect money early. Use apps like Venmo or Splitwise to track who paid for what and settle up easily.

Communicate Often

Keep everyone informed with regular updates. Create a group chat for easy communication and quick questions.

Build in Buffer Time

Don't pack the schedule too tightly. Allow time for people to run late, activities to go long, and spontaneous fun to happen.

Plan for Safety

Arrange designated drivers or rideshare options. Keep emergency contacts handy. Assign a "mom friend" role to stay relatively sober.

Common Planning Mistakes to Avoid

Waiting Too Long to Book

Popular entertainers, venues, and accommodations book up months in advance, especially during peak wedding season.

Not Discussing Budget Early

Money conversations can be awkward, but being upfront prevents resentment and financial stress later.

Over-Planning Every Minute

Leave room for spontaneity and relaxation. Sometimes the best memories come from unplanned moments.

Ignoring the Bride's Preferences

This celebration is for her. Make sure your plans align with what she'll actually enjoy, not what you think she should want.

Ready to Start Planning?

With this comprehensive checklist, you're equipped to plan an exceptional bachelorette celebration that runs smoothly from start to finish. Remember that the goal is creating joyful memories with the bride and her closest friends. Stay organized, communicate clearly, and don't forget to enjoy the experience yourself.

The most successful bachelorette parties balance careful planning with flexibility, include activities everyone can enjoy, and keep the bride's happiness as the top priority. Use this checklist as your roadmap, adapt it to your specific situation, and get ready for an unforgettable celebration.
